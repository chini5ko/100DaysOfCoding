I was born on planet Earth, Margarita Island, Venezuela. Actually, my parents and their ancestors are from China but traveled a couple of continents settling down in South America as the Spanish did in 1450. Thanks to the Spanish, I learned their language that was passed down after their colonization in Venezuela. On the island, I spoke to my parents in Cantonese, which language has more than 3000 years of history. In history class, I realized that just in uptown America (North America), those people speak English. Living the language of English in New York City, I started my college adventure!

As you can see, I am studying at Columbia University in the City of New York the Major of Computer Science. Also, I have an associate degree in Business Administration that I received in BMCC. I chose these two majors because as a kid I saw the intersection between technology and business when my parents implemented a computer to register their products; thus, the store productivity increases thanks to technology.

Knowing the computer history, which is very short, I am open to exploring more technology fields besides business.
